I am a senior undergraduate student studying Computer Sciences and Psychology at University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am applying to PhD programs in Computer and Information Sciences for Fall 2023.

My research interests lie in human-computer interaction (HCI), social computing, and human-AI interaction. I am interested in improving online discourse and countering problematic user behavior and user-generated content in online social platforms by building and studying social systems to 1) reimagine the design of platform features, 2) apply behavioral interventions, and 3) support community-centered human-AI moderation. I seek to improve the platform and governance design of online social platforms to build healthier online information environments.

I am currently working with Prof. Jacob Thebault-Spieker to study how system design shapes user-generated content in peer production and social media platforms. I have also worked with Prof. Amy Ogan at Carnegie Mellon University on using classroom sensing systems as professional development tools to improve teaching and facilitate behavior change.
